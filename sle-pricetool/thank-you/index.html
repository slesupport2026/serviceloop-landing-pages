Pricing Your Services

Why Pricing is THE Biggest Mistake

Everyone in your business, outside your business, and around your business are on a journey through life, conflict, and conflict management. Electricians are no exception!

Every Electripreneur on the planet has this journey in common to become an electrician:

  1. 1.Decided that Electrical is or could be a great trade
  2. 2.Got a job & received technical theoretical training
  3. 3.Field pressure to NOT make mistakes and to get quicker!
  4. 4.Became licensed in their trade for their area
  5. 5.Realized how much they don't know
  6. 6.Get confident in what they do know
  7. 7.Realize either an Opportunity or Conflict to go on their own

At no point did they receive business, accounting, marketing, or sales training.

  1. 8.Open a Business
  2. 9.Choose some branding
  3. 10.Get to work with some clients they know
  4. 11.Realize they DO NOT know what to charge?
  • a.So, they call around and pretend to be a customer...
  • b.Learning what others charge... They charge a little less with the intent to do a little bit more. That's valuable, right?

Positioning yourself as a low-cost provider is a strategy, but without training and actual supporting business model... it's death by 1000 cuts.

The correct way to establish pricing and market positioning is to ENSURE that what we're doing is coming through the numbers. IF we don't yet have the numbers, then the exercise that is needed is to PROJECT using industry experience.

Luckily, we're here for you with that exact experience and a formula for your math.

Rules for Pricing
"The Casino Principal: The House Always Wins. Every business needs this strength."
  1. 1 If you don't know your "average efficiency" of direct vs indirect labor hours, then assume 50%.
  2. 2 Use a 30% Markup to start (Result is ~23% margin).
  3. 3 Price NEVER goes down. (Only up) — growth/inflation.
  4. 4 Staff Variable = Avg # of working crew service vehicles.
  5. 5 Add transaction fees to your current expenses. Avg the amount or assume 50% of customers will use CC if data does not exist (CC Fee 2-3% x 50% x Projected Annual Sales Revenue). Don't charge clients fees.
  6. 6 The annual price increase should be 5% + %Inflation in your area.
  7. 7 Add 2% losses of total projected revenue to expenses IF you have a Premium Service Position with guarantees.
  8. 8 Review Quarterly to maintain good positioning health.
